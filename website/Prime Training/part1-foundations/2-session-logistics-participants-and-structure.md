## The Prime Team (as presented)

The trainer walked through the Prime organization slide: Jay/James oversees the function; Mark (architect/developer) and Sean (architect) provide technical leadership; four engineers carry the development workload; and Nadine serves as Scrum Master. The team's small size was noted repeatedly as context for its development philosophy — features ship fast because there is no UI layer to build, but initiatives like static code scanning have historically lacked bandwidth (a purchased scanning license was never operationalized). The team indicated openness to AI-assisted development, noting an internal Copilot license is available, and took the AI/DevOps pipeline conversation offline.

## Instructional Approach

The trainer emphasized a troubleshooting-first learning philosophy — memorably offering to *"come to your lab and break something you have to figure out"* — and structured the day around a live build rather than slides alone. The engagement deck contains roughly 140 slides; the group agreed to selective coverage, prioritizing the demo, the configuration deep dives, and the tools most relevant to imminent customer work (Bulk Update, WPI, self-service protection).
